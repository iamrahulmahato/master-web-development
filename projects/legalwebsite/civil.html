Civil Law

Civil law encompasses a wide range of legal issues that do not involve criminal offenses. It deals with disputes between individuals, organizations, or entities seeking compensation or resolution for various grievances.

Common areas of civil law include contract disputes, personal injury cases, family law matters such as divorce and child custody, real estate disputes, and more. In civil law cases, the goal is to provide remedies, such as monetary compensation or specific performance, to the injured party.

Our team of experienced civil attorneys is dedicated to helping clients navigate the complexities of civil litigation. We work to protect our clients' rights and interests while seeking favorable outcomes through negotiation, mediation, or courtroom representation.

Indian Contract Act, 1872

The Indian Contract Act, 1872, defines the legal framework for contracts and agreements in India, including their formation, validity, and enforcement.

The Transfer of Property Act, 1882

This law governs the transfer of property in India and regulates transactions involving immovable property such as land and buildings.

Hindu Marriage Act, 1955

The Hindu Marriage Act governs the marriage laws for Hindus, providing regulations for marriage ceremonies, registration, and divorce procedures.

Indian Succession Act, 1925

This law deals with matters related to the succession of property, wills, and intestate succession in India.

Consumer Protection Act, 2019

The Consumer Protection Act safeguards the rights and interests of consumers in India, addressing issues related to consumer complaints and disputes.
